JEE Main vs JEE Advanced

Know which exam suits your goals and how to plan your attempt strategy.

At a glance

Aspect JEE Main JEE Advanced
Purpose NITs, IIITs, GFTIs admissions; Advanced qualifier IIT admissions only
Difficulty Moderate; NCERT-aligned High; concept-intensive, multi-step
Pattern Objective MCQs & numerical Varied: MCQ, numerical, partial marking
Attempts Multiple sessions per year Max 2 attempts in consecutive years
Eligibility 12th appearing/passed Top rankers in JEE Main, class XII criteria
Syllabus coverage Class XI–XII standard Deeper, application-heavy

Preparation approach

  • Main: speed + accuracy on standard patterns. Practice chapter-wise, then mock tests.
  • Advanced: train for multi-concept problems, partial marking logic, and novel formats.
  • For IIT target: use Advanced-level problem sets even during Main prep after basics.

Seat outcomes

  • JEE Main good rank → NIT/IIIT/GFTI with strong branches.
  • JEE Advanced good rank → IITs; branch choice heavily depends on rank tier.

Tip: Don’t overthink which is “harder”—align preparation with your target colleges and test patterns.
