Career Planning Guide for High School Students

This guide helps you understand your interests, explore career options, pick the right subjects, and plan college entrance exams. Use it as a checklist while you progress through classes 9–12.

1) Know Yourself

  • Identify subjects you enjoy and perform well in (PCM, PCB, Commerce, Humanities).
  • List your strengths: logical reasoning, creativity, communication, empathy, analysis.
  • Take an aptitude/interest test and talk to mentors or teachers.

2) Explore Careers

  • Engineering: Software, Mechanical, Electrical, Civil, etc. Entrance: JEE Main/Advanced.
  • Medical: MBBS, BDS, AYUSH, Nursing, Paramedical. Entrance: NEET.
  • Commerce: CA, CS, CMA, BBA, Finance/Marketing/Analytics.
  • Arts/Humanities: Law (CLAT), Psychology, Design (NID/NIFT), Journalism, Literature.
  • Science (Non-medical): Data Science, Research, Biotechnology, Pure Sciences.

3) Choose Subjects Strategically

  • Engineering: Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics (PCM).
  • Medical: Physics, Chemistry, Biology (PCB).
  • Commerce: Accountancy, Business Studies, Economics, Mathematics (optional).
  • Arts: Psychology, Political Science, History, Sociology, Literature.

4) Entrance Exams and Timelines

  • JEE Main: 11–12th syllabus; Advanced for IITs.
  • NEET UG: PCB syllabus for MBBS/BDS/AYUSH.
  • CLAT: For BA LLB programs.
  • NID/NIFT: For Design and Fashion programs.
Tip: Create a 12-month study plan with weekly targets. Practice previous year papers and mock tests.

5) Build a Profile

  • Projects: coding apps, research posters, design portfolios, community work.
  • Competitions: Olympiads, hackathons, quizzes, debates, arts.
  • Internships/Shadowing: local clinics, startups, NGOs, research labs.

6) Shortlist Colleges

  • Government Institutes: IITs, NITs, AIIMS, DU, etc.
  • Private Universities: BITS, VIT, SRM, Symbiosis, etc.
  • Compare fees, placements, faculty, location, course structure.
