NEET Preparation Tips

Ace NEET with a practical, time-tested plan covering syllabus mastery, practice, and revision.

Exam focus: Biology (50%), Chemistry (25%), Physics (25%). Prioritize NCERT for Bio and Chem; build conceptual clarity for Physics.

1) Syllabus-first strategy

  • Complete NCERT for Biology line-by-line; mark diagrams, definitions, and exemplar questions.
  • For Chemistry, finish NCERT (Inorganic/Organic basics). Use a standard guide for Physical practice.
  • Physics: concept > formula. Maintain a mistake log of formula derivations and common traps.

2) Daily study rhythm (6–8 hrs)

Morning (Concept)

2–3 hrs focused reading of NCERT/notes. Convert key ideas into flashcards.

Noon (Practice)

2–3 hrs chapter-wise MCQs. Target 120–150 questions/day split across subjects.

Evening (Revision)

1–2 hrs spaced repetition. Revisit your mistake log and weak topics.

3) Practice pipeline

  • Start with chapter-wise MCQs; move to mixed-topic tests after 60–70% coverage.
  • Give full-length tests weekly at first, then 2–3 per week in the last 8 weeks.
  • After each test, allocate equal time to analysis: why each wrong answer happened and how to fix it.

4) High-yield topics

  • Biology: Human Physiology, Genetics, Ecology, Plant Physiology, Reproduction.
  • Chemistry: Chemical Bonding, Thermodynamics, Equilibrium, Organic mechanisms, Coordination.
  • Physics: Mechanics, Electrostatics, Current Electricity, Optics, Modern Physics.

5) Last-month booster

  • Alternate days: one full test + review. Keep a rapid-revision stack of 200–300 flashcards.
  • Attempt previous 10 years’ NEET papers in exam-like conditions.
  • Sleep 7–8 hrs; maintain light exercise for focus and stamina.

6) Test-day checklist

  • Reach early with ID, admit card, and permitted stationery.
  • First pass: attempt sure-shot questions; second pass: moderate; third pass: time-permitting tough ones.
  • Keep a steady pace. If stuck beyond 60–75 seconds, mark and move.

Tip: Your mistake log is your biggest rank booster—update it daily.
